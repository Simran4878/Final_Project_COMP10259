Introduction

The blue tang is very small, cute and bright coloured fish native of the coral reefs mostly found in the seas and oceans. Blue tangs are also called by the name of hippo tang, regal blue tang and sometimes with the blue surgeonfish because all these species looks similar to each other. A very famous Disney character "Dory" in the movies such as "Finding Nemo" and "Finding Dory" is based on the Blue Tang.

Appearance

  • Blue tangs have royal blue body colour with a yellow tale.
  • They can grown up to 10 or 12 inches.
  • Blue tangs are also specified as a surgeonfish because of scalpel like spines
  • Body contains a black palette design.
  • Male tangs are larger than female ones.
  • They have a oval shaped body

Habitat

  • Inhibit coral reefs and inshore the rocky areas in the seas.
  • They prefer the depth between 6 to 130 feet under the water.
  • Mostly found in the countries like Japan, Philippines, Indonesia and Australia
  • Blue tangs like small crevices and holes within the reef.

Diet & Behavior

  • Blue Tangs are mostly herbivores
  • Blue tangs feed on algae and plankton
  • In case of emergency, they play dead by laying on the side of the rocks.
  • Their spines are very sharp.
  • In mature age, Blue Tangs enjoy reef surface.

Life Span and reproduction

An average age of these species is around 30 years or more but only a few of them even survive for 20 years. They reproduce through a process called broadcast spawning. Males release sperm into the column of the water and females releases the eggs. The number of eggs per session is 40,000. After spawning they settle in a specific reef for the transformation.

Fun Facts

  1. Blue tangs are capable of adjusting the intensity of their colour from blue to purple.
  2. Sometimes they are also found in the swimming pools.
  3. The flesh of the blue tang is poisonous.
  4. Blue tang becomes famous by the Disney movies.
  5. The blue tang is not always blue.
